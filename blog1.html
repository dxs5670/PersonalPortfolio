Mitigating Bias in Our Algorithms

By Dylan Stetts | April 14, 2020


Today, we use algorithms to do everything for us. From simple things like Netflix recommendations to risk assessments for expected recidivism, using computers to estimate human behavior is ingrained in our life.

Many people think that there is less potential bias because there is no human making decisions. Is this truly the case?


The Morals of Algorithms

While “Algorithms by themselves don't have a moral character,” they inherently take on the values of those who develop them, or rather those who's data are modeled by the algorithm.

As Google denotes, latent bias exists when preexisting stereotypes are modeled. This risk is twofold: not only is this bias harder to detect because it isn't coming from a human, but it is also more difficult to regulate. Essentially, we are allowing historical biases, like racism, work their way into our predictive software.
