Who is Dylan Stetts?

“I’m the type of person that if you ask a question and I don’t know the answer, I’ll tell you I don’t know. But I know how to find the answer and I will find the answer.” -Chris Gardner

I love solving puzzles. As a kid, I remember playing Tetris for hours at a time, finding new ways to fit blocks together. Tetris became the building block for my passion for technology.

By the time I was 8, I built my first computer with spare parts I collected with my grandmother at yard sales. Although impressed, my mom would still yell at me when she caught me tearing down her computer. I always made sure to put it back just the way it was, even when I borrowed some of her parts for a couple days.

Before too long, I became my family’s dedicated technician. Whether it is a bad circuit or a buggy operating system, I will do everything I can to break down and diagnose the problem. Once, when my Dad’s laptop wouldn’t turn on, I convinced him to let me use the oven to adjust the solder on his motherboard. He was skeptical, but very enthusiastic when it finally turned on after reassembly.

Now, I am even more fascinated by what technology can do. Penn State has given me the opportunity to explore the power of software. I understand how impactful different technologies can be, especially when they work together. I am inspired by the ability of tech to improve lives at a scale never before realized in the world’s history. My dream is to build systems that empower people, solve challenges, and make the world a better place.
